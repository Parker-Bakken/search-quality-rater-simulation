# Search Result Evaluation Checklist

When rating search results, the following factors should be considered.

---

## Intent Match
Does the result answer what the user is trying to accomplish?

Example intents:
• informational
• navigational
• transactional
• local

---

## Usefulness
Does the page provide actionable or meaningful information?

Consider:
• clarity
• completeness
• relevance

---

## Trustworthiness
Is the source credible?

Indicators:
• recognized authority
• references or citations
• secure domain
• lack of spam signals

---

## Effort Required
How much effort is required for the user to solve their problem?

Factors:
• number of clicks
• scrolling
• unnecessary steps

---

## Freshness
Is up-to-date information required for this query?

Examples where freshness matters:
• technology
• pricing
• news
• events

---

## YMYL Sensitivity
Queries related to health, finance, or legal issues require higher trust standards.

Examples:
• medical advice
• financial guidance
• legal information
